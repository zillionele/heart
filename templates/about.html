About This Project

Learn more about our heart disease prediction system, how it works, and its limitations.

How It Works

This heart disease prediction system uses a machine learning model built with scikit-learn to analyze heart rate and blood glucose data. The model processes these inputs to estimate the potential risk of heart disease based on patterns it has learned from medical data.

The system uses a neural network architecture that has been trained on synthetic data for demonstration purposes. In a real-world application, the model would be trained on extensive clinical data from patients with known heart disease outcomes.

When you input your heart rate and blood glucose values, the system:

  1. Preprocesses your data by scaling it to a range the model can work with
  2. Passes the processed data through the neural network
  3. Obtains a probability score representing the estimated risk
  4. Categorizes the risk as Low, Moderate, or High based on the probability
  5. Presents the results with visualizations to help you understand the assessment

Limitations

While our system provides a risk assessment based on heart rate and blood glucose, it's important to understand that heart disease risk depends on many more factors, including:

  • Age, gender, and family history
  • Blood pressure and cholesterol levels
  • Smoking status and alcohol consumption
  • Physical activity levels and diet
  • Presence of other conditions like diabetes
Important: This tool is for educational purposes only and should never replace professional medical advice or diagnosis.

The Science

Heart Rate

Resting heart rate is an indicator of cardiovascular health. Studies have shown that elevated resting heart rates may be associated with increased risk of cardiovascular events. A normal resting heart rate for adults typically ranges from 60 to 100 beats per minute, with lower rates generally indicating better cardiovascular fitness.

Blood Glucose

Elevated blood glucose levels are associated with diabetes, which is a significant risk factor for heart disease. Normal fasting blood glucose levels typically range from 70 to 100 mg/dL. Levels above 126 mg/dL may indicate diabetes, while levels between 100 and 125 mg/dL suggest prediabetes.

While these two factors provide valuable information, comprehensive risk assessment requires evaluation of multiple factors by healthcare professionals.

Technical Implementation

This application is built using the following technologies:

  • Flask: A lightweight Python web framework for serving the application
  • scikit-learn: A machine learning library for Python used to build and train the neural network model
  • Bootstrap: A CSS framework for building responsive, mobile-first websites
  • Chart.js: A JavaScript library for creating interactive data visualizations

The model used in this application is a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) neural network with two hidden layers. It takes the heart rate and blood glucose values as input and outputs a probability representing the estimated risk of heart disease.
